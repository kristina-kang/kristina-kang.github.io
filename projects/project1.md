# Cyclistic Bike Share Analysis

## Project Overview

This case study analyzes how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic's bike-sharing service differently.

The goal was to identify behavioral patterns and provide data-driven recommendations to help convert casual riders into annual members.

**Tools:** SQL, Excel, Tableau, Kaggle

---

## Business Problem

Cyclistic's marketing team wanted to understand the differences between casual riders and annual members.

The key question:

**How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently, and how can these insights support membership growth?**

---

## Data Analysis Process

### 1. Data Preparation

- Combined historical bike trip data
- Cleaned missing and duplicate records
- Created new variables including ride duration and day-of-week analysis

### 2. Exploratory Analysis

Analyzed:

- Ride frequency by membership type
- Ride duration patterns
- Weekly and seasonal usage trends
- Bike type preferences

---

## Key Insights

### Casual riders take longer rides

Casual riders generally had longer average ride durations compared to annual members, suggesting their usage was more leisure-oriented.

### Annual members show consistent usage patterns

Members used Cyclistic bikes more regularly throughout the week, indicating stronger commuting and routine-based behavior.

### Peak usage periods differ by rider type

Usage patterns revealed differences in when casual riders and members were most active.
