# Cyclistic Bike Share Analysis

## Project Overview

This case study analyzes how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic's bike-sharing service differently.

The goal was to identify rider behavior patterns and provide data-driven recommendations to help increase annual memberships.

**Tools:** SQL, Excel, Tableau, Kaggle

---

## Business Problem

Cyclistic's marketing team wanted to better understand the differences between casual riders and annual members.

The key business question:

**How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently, and how can these insights support membership growth?**

---

## Data Analysis Process

### Data Preparation

- Combined historical bike trip data
- Cleaned missing and inconsistent records
- Removed duplicate entries
- Created calculated fields including ride duration and weekday analysis

### Exploratory Analysis

Analyzed rider behavior based on:

- Membership type
- Ride duration
- Usage frequency
- Day-of-week trends
- Seasonal patterns
- Bike type preferences

---

## Key Insights

### Casual riders take longer rides

Casual riders generally had longer ride durations, suggesting their trips were more leisure-oriented.

### Annual members show consistent usage patterns

Annual members demonstrated more regular usage throughout the week, indicating routine-based commuting behavior.

### Rider behavior differs by time and season

Usage patterns varied between rider groups, creating opportunities for targeted marketing strategies.

## Recommendations

- Create targeted campaigns for casual riders
- Promote annual membership benefits during high leisure-use periods
- Introduce incentives that encourage casual riders to become annual members
